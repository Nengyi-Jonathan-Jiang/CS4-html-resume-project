My favorite projects

  • Go Capy Go! Game Mobile Friendly

    A retro-style platformer/puzzle game. Get the capybara to the candy! I made this with the help of Eva Li, Rounak Rai, and Santiago Espinoza for a local game jam.

  • Music Analyzer Tool

    A tool I made to help me transcribe music (figure out the notes from an audio recording). It shows you the spectrum of the audio as it plays and tries to identify peaks (which indicate a note or a harmonic of a note that might be played). I have found that it has tripled my efficiency at transcribing music, as it helps me identify softer notes in complex chords that my ear might have missed. Built with Next.js

  • Reaction-Diffusion simulator Simulation

    A really cool simulation that I worked on. You can control the reaction parameters (reaction rate, diffusion radius, diffusion rates, feed rate, remove rate, etc.) and the speed of the simulation (steps per frame). For some combinations of parameters, complex structures reminiscent of cellular division can occur. I used compute shaders in WebGL to calculate the steps of the simulation.

  • Mushroom Mania Game Mobile Friendly

    A retro-style game based on a game from coolmathgames.com. Match three mushrooms to make a bigger mushroom. A and D to move the mushrooms, W to rotate, S to drop.

  • Klotski (华容道) GameMobile Friendly

    A chinese sliding puzzle. Get the big piece labeled 曹 to the bottom. These puzzles are harder than they look — many require 50+ moves, with the last one requiring more than 130!

  • Three body simulator Simulation

    Another really cool simulation that I worked on. Simulates three massive stars in 3d space interacting through gravity.

  • Five in a row (Gomoku, 五子棋) GameMobile Friendly

    A classic board game. Get five stones in a row! The AI I made for this (using just pattern recognition, no neural networks or even tree search) is hard, but beatable.

  • Light Puzzle Game Simulation Algorithm Mobile Friendly

    An interesting puzzle. You start with a random grid of lights that may be on or off, and can toggle all the lights in any 3x3 square centered on a light in the grid. The goal is to turn all the lights on. I wrote a solver for the general case using a bit of linear algebra.

  • Display Math Tool

    Math display thing I made, a bit similar to LaTeX.

  • SVG editor Tool

    Edit the raw XML of an SVG. Has rudimentary syntax highlighting and real-time preview. You can save SVGs to your computer as an svg file or png file.

  • Circle good, Square bad GameMobile Friendly

    A bullet hell game. Eat the circles and avoid everything else! Best played on a touchscreen device, although mouse is okay. Touchpad highly discouraged.

  • Maze Game GameMobile Friendly

    A maze game featuring keys and obstacles. All levels are randomly generated, so some may be easier than others. However, all are guaranteed to be solvable.

  • Space Junk Game

    One of the first HTML/CSS/JS games I wrote. You control a spaceship with arrow keys and avoid the junk and collect golden hearts.
